Why this matters

Stronger businesses. Smarter leaders. A better roofing industry.

As the roofing industry grows more competitive and complex, the Carolinas Roofing Association helps members adapt, build relationships, and make smarter decisions that strengthen their businesses.

For more than eight decades, the association has been the regional gathering point for the Carolinas roofing community — a place where contractors compare notes, where vendors and manufacturers meet the decision-makers, and where the next generation of roofing leadership learns from the operators who came before them.

What members get out of it

The association's job is to connect you to the people, programs, and proof that make running a Carolinas roofing business easier.

  1. Owner-to-owner relationships across both states.
  2. Practical education across business, safety, and sales pillars.
  3. Self-Insurers Fund eligibility for qualifying contractors.
  4. Year-round events, district meetings, the Annual Golf Tournament, and the Carolinas Roofing Expo.
  5. A trusted regional voice on industry issues.

The honest case for membership

The cost of going it alone in Carolinas roofing.

Running a roofing business in NC or SC is hard enough. Doing it without a regional network behind you is harder than it needs to be.

Without a network

You miss what the rest of the industry already knows.

Regulatory updates, code changes, workforce trends, manufacturer shifts, regional market moves — your competitors are hearing it from each other. You're hearing it last.

Without peers

You're making big calls with no one to call.

Pricing pressure, hiring decisions, equipment investments, hard projects — running them past someone who's been there changes outcomes. Members have that someone.

Without a voice

You have no regional standing where it matters.

Regulators, code bodies, workforce boards, and statewide partners listen to organized industries. A regional voice for Carolinas roofing isn't optional — it's how the industry stays seen.

Why roofing professionals choose membership

Membership in the Carolinas Roofing Association is built around the way roofing businesses actually grow — through trusted relationships, practical education, and a stronger regional voice. Whether you operate a residential crew in Charlotte, run a commercial roofing company in Columbia, or supply the industry across both states, membership connects you to the people, programs, and conversations that move your business forward.

Who can join

The association welcomes professionals across four core categories:

  • Residential roofing contractors serving homeowners across the Carolinas.
  • Commercial roofing contractors working on industrial, institutional, and multi-family projects.
  • Vendors, manufacturers, and suppliers who serve the regional roofing industry.
  • Service providers and industry partners — software, insurance, legal, financial, training, and specialty service firms.

What membership includes

  • Access to year-round events, education sessions, and the Carolinas Roofing Expo & Education.
  • Member directory listing for business visibility across the Carolinas.
  • Education resources covering safety, business operations, sales, workforce, legal, and technical topics.
  • Regulatory and industry updates relevant to North and South Carolina.
  • Eligibility for member programs, including the Self-Insurers Fund (SIF) where qualifications apply.
  • Sponsorship, exhibiting, and partnership opportunities throughout the year.
  • Owner-to-owner relationships with leaders across the regional roofing industry.

How membership works

  1. Submit your application. Tell us about your company, your role, and your membership category.
  2. Connect with the association. A team member will follow up to confirm details and welcome you to the network.
  3. Start participating. Attend events including the Carolinas Roofing Expo, get listed in the member directory, and engage with education, resources, and regional relationships available to members.

Member benefits

Membership delivers practical, year-round value for Carolinas roofing professionals:

  • Access to the CRA Self-Insurers Fund workers' compensation program
  • Participation in a health insurance program for employees and family members
  • Use of the CRA facility for conference room and training space needs
  • Education credits to support business needs and meet OSHA requirements
  • Affinity Partner discounts on services for your business
  • Access to legal counsel and legal assistance
  • Sample contracts, forms, and guarantee forms
  • Networking with roofing industry professionals to learn and share best practices
  • Roofing and safety training and seminars
  • The Carolinas Contacts publication
  • Safety program templates
  • Referrals
  • Listing in the CRA Members Directory
  • Legislative and regulatory issue support

Membership categories & requirements

Contractors

Roofing-performing companies. Requirements may include one of the following:

  • A roofing contractor license number issued by North Carolina or South Carolina
  • A valid business and/or privilege license issued by North Carolina or South Carolina
  • In South Carolina, both a valid business license and a Residential Specialty Contractor License

Associates

Includes vendor/supplier, manufacturer, distributor, and independent representative categories. No listed requirements.

Sustaining

Service providers to contractor companies or any other categories. No listed requirements.

Approval process

All applicants require CRA Board member review and majority approval.

CRA Self-Insurers Fund

The CRA Self-Insurers Fund is the oldest workers' compensation group fund in the Carolinas and could help eligible members save money. Participation is reserved for qualifying members. Full eligibility requirements and a workers' compensation contact are on the dedicated Self-Insurers Fund page.

Member Program · Workers' Compensation

The CRA Self-Insurers Fund

The CRA Self-Insurers Fund is the oldest workers' compensation group fund in the Carolinas — and it could be saving you money.

Request a Quote Become a Member

A long-running workers' compensation program for Carolinas roofing contractors

The CRA Self-Insurers Fund is the oldest workers' compensation group fund in the Carolinas. As a member program, it gives qualifying roofing contractors access to group workers' compensation coverage and could help eligible companies save money.

Group membership approval requirements

Group membership approval is contingent on the following:

  • Approval of membership in the Carolinas Roofing Association, Inc.
  • Completion of the applications (competitor and credit references are important)
  • A favorable Loss Control Survey
  • Anticipated payroll by class code in order to produce a proposal
  • Three (3) years in business with prior workers' compensation coverage information
  • A minimum net worth of $25,000 (required by the South Carolina Workers' Compensation Commission)
  • A minimum annual premium of $15,000
  • Annual premiums are payable monthly through self-audit, with 1/6 of the estimated annual premium placed in an escrow deposit at inception

Education pillars

The association's education programming is organized around the topics that matter most to roofing business owners and operators across North and South Carolina.

  • Safety & Risk — practical safety training, risk-reduction practices, and worksite leadership for residential and commercial crews.
  • Business Operations — financial fundamentals, project management, scheduling, and operational efficiency for roofing companies.
  • Sales & Estimating — modern sales practices, estimating discipline, and customer experience for residential and commercial work.
  • Workforce Development — recruiting, retention, training pipelines, and emerging leader development.
  • Legal & Regulatory — guidance on regulations, licensing, contracts, and compliance issues across the Carolinas.

About · Since 1943

About the Carolinas Roofing Association

A regional trade association serving roofing professionals across North and South Carolina — connecting contractors, vendors, manufacturers, and service providers through education, events, and industry leadership since 1943.

About CRA

The Carolinas Roofing Association (CRA) — formerly the Carolinas Roofing & Sheet Metal Contractors Association — was formed in 1943 by a group of contractors. The main objective for CRA is to promote and safeguard the common business interests of its members and to improve business conditions by educating all persons concerning the roofing and sheet metal business and industry, and to support the development and progress of the roofing and sheet metal industry in the Carolinas.

Our mission

To promote and safeguard the common business interests of its members and to improve conditions by educating all persons concerning the roofing and sheet metal business and industry. To work for the development and progress of the roofing and sheet metal business industry and to work with individuals' organizations and governmental agencies toward the achievement of a stronger profession of the roofing and sheet metal industry.

Who we serve

Membership consists of licensed roofing contractors in North and South Carolina, along with Associate and Sustaining members. Associate members supply products to our contractor members, and Sustaining members supply services. CRA's contractor membership is divided into 10 districts, and district meetings are held throughout the year so roofing contractors can address developments in their area.

Events throughout the year

CRA hosts a variety of events throughout the year to engage members, potential members, and industry peers — providing networking and education. These include (but are not limited to) trade shows and expositions, the annual meeting, district meetings, golf tournaments, and sporting clays competitions.

The strength of this association comes from the members who are willing to donate their time and talents to keep CRA growing and improve services to the membership.

Leadership

CRA is led by a volunteer Executive Team and supported by association staff. Meet the people who lead the association:

  • Board Members — Executive Team & CRA staff
  • Past Presidents — those who have led CRA since 1943

Qualifications & bylaws

Qualifications for Membership

Pursuant to item (j) of the By-Laws of the Carolinas Roofing Association (CRA), the Board of Directors adopted the following rules affecting qualifications for membership in CRA. These standards for Membership in CRA shall apply prospectively from the date of adoption.

  • Applicant must be a roofing, sheet metal, waterproofing, roof deck, insulation, or siding contractor who is licensed by either North Carolina or South Carolina and who has established a reputation for skill, integrity and responsibility.
    • Residential Contractor Member in North Carolina must possess a business and/or privilege license.
    • Residential Contractor Member in South Carolina must possess a business and a residential specialty license.
  • Applicant must be domiciled in North Carolina or South Carolina.
  • Residential Contractor applicant must provide current insurance carrier for the following:
    • General Liability
    • Workers' Compensation
  • Applicant must agree to pay the annual membership dues and must include the said membership dues with the application.
  • By majority vote of the CRA Board of Directors, suspension of these rules affecting qualifications for membership in CRA and acceptance of the application may be granted based on the following criteria:
    • At least three (3) years cumulative experience in the above-mentioned areas of construction, and
    • Documented expertise in the above-mentioned areas of construction.

Maintenance of Membership

I. Suspension of Membership. Membership in CRA may be suspended by a majority vote of the CRA Board of Directors upon the following grounds:

  • Indictment for a crime involving moral turpitude.
  • Indictment for a crime which constitutes a felony.

Suspension from membership in CRA does not affect the requirement of the Workers' Compensation Insurance Plan that one may be a member in good standing of CRA-SIF.

II. Termination of Membership.

  • Any member in good standing may resign from CRA at the end of the fiscal year, provided the company has given written notice of intention to do so at least 90 days prior to the end of the fiscal year.
  • In the event that arrearages of annual membership dues remain unpaid for a period of three (3) months, the CRA Board of Directors is authorized to terminate membership.
  • The CRA Board of Directors is authorized to terminate membership in the event a member becomes bankrupt, insolvent, or there is an assignment for the benefit of creditors.
  • Membership may be terminated by the CRA Board of Directors upon conviction of a crime involving moral turpitude or of a crime constituting a felony.
  • Upon complaint and hearing, any member of CRA found guilty of a violation of the By-Laws, or of conduct on his/her part likely to, in the opinion of CRA, injure its welfare, interest and reputation, may be expelled by the CRA Board of Directors.
  • The CRA Board of Directors has authority to expel any member whose license is suspended or revoked by the State agency.
  • Upon termination of membership in CRA for any cause, all of the rights, title and interest of such membership cease and revert back to CRA.

Adopted 09/04/1985. Revised 03/2023.
